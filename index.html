Here's a timeline of Charles Babbage's life

  • 1791 - Born in London, England
  • 1812 - Founded the Analytical Society
  • 1816 - Elected a fellow of the Royal Society of London
  • 1823 - Obtained government support for the Difference Engine, which had a 20-decimal capacity
  • 1828 - Starts teaching Mathematics at University of Cambridge
  • 1833 - Design and construction of the Difference Engine ceases, after the machinist refused to continue until he was prepaid
  • mid-1830s - Develops plans for the Analytical Engine, the forerunner of the modern digital computer
  • 1839 - Stops teaching at University of Cambridge
  • 1871 - Passes away at the age of 79, in London, England
